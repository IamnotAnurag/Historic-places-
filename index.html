Historical Places in India

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal; it also houses the tomb of Shah Jahan himself. The Taj Mahal is widely considered to be one of the most beautiful buildings in the world and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The Red Fort is a historic fort in the city of Delhi in India. It was the main residence of the emperors of the Mughal dynasty for nearly 200 years, until 1857. The Red Fort complex is named after its massive red sandstone walls and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is known for its architectural grandeur and houses several important structures like the Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, and the famous Lahori Gate.

The Ellora Caves are a series of rock-cut caves located in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India. They are known for their unique architecture, which represents a fusion of Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain traditions. The caves were carved out of basaltic rock during the 6th to 10th centuries and contain elaborate sculptures, frescoes, and carvings. The Ellora Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and are a testament to the rich cultural heritage of India.
